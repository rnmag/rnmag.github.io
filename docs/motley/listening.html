Mahler, 6th Symphony “Tragic”

At first glance, this symphony’s overall shape is similar to Beethoven’s 9th: charged first movement, angry scherzo, hymn-like slow movement and a crazy, unwieldy finale. But while Beethoven finishes his piece in triumph, Mahler ends his with crushing blows.

This analogy only works with the Scherzo-Andante order for the inner movements, which is controversial. Since Mahler himself seemed to go back and forth, I see no sense in being stubbornly attached to one specific order. That being said, I have consistently favored Scherzo-Andante recordings, and I’m afraid my enjoyment of the piece is hopelessly tied to this sequence at this point. The calm of the Andante fits perfectly as a much-needed relief after the boisterous Scherzo. On the other hand, there’s much less need of relief after the optimistic ending of the first movement.

Whatever the order, the Andante is among my favorite of Mahler’s slow movements, along with the finales of the 3rd and 9th symphonies. Overall, I love the variety of moods in the piece: there are military marches, cheerful celebrations, alpine cowbells, welcoming embraces. And hammer blows, of course.

Rankings

Ranking works of art is an exercise best left to fools. So here we are.

Top 10 Symphonies

# Composer Piece Note
1 Mahler 9th Symphony The first movement is the epitome of Mahler’s motto: “Embrace Everything”. The music that follows reacts to it by expressing irony, bitterness, and, finally, acceptance.
2 Shostakovich 8th Symphony After 45 minutes of distress, violence, and gloom, the key change introducing the last movement feels like waking up from a nightmare.
3 Beethoven 9th Symphony This level of ambition and execution would only be matched (but not surpassed) generations later by Mahler’s 2nd.
4 Mahler 6th Symphony A masterpiece of contrasts: military march, sunshine, pastoral contemplation and kindness. But it all ends with hammer blows and heartbreak.
5 Shostakovich 10th Symphony A diffident artist prevails over authoritarian oppression.
6 Bartók Music for Strings, Percussion and Celesta Dark fugues, dense counterpoint, creepy night music, folksy celebration. Perfect for those days when you crave a spicy dish.
7 Tchaikovsky 6th Symphony Gorgeous melodies, and the last movement can be unbearable if you’re feeling down. Tchaikovsky doesn’t get enough respect for this achievement.
8 Adams Harmonielehre Minimalism meets the late romantic style. I will always associate it with Civ IV.
9 Sibelius 2nd Symphony Since I first heard the opening chords back in high school, I knew I would love this music forever.
10 Shostakovich 4th Symphony Aggressive dissonances, raging outbursts, harrowing fugue. Heavy metal with an orchestra.

Top 10 Chamber Music

# Composer Piece Note
1 Schubert Cello Quintet In late Schubert, trills always serve as a harbinger of something ominous
2 Mendelssohn String Octet Energy, inspiration and balance without any weak points
3 Schoenberg Verklarte Nacht Immense buildup of tension followed by a release of compassion and understanding
