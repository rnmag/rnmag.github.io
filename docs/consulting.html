Consulting

Since 2018, I have led an independent statistical consulting practice, partnering with organizations to solve complex data challenges. My engagements range from highly targeted analytical deep-dives to multi-year architectural commitments.

While the tools I use are highly technical—primarily Bayesian inference and probabilistic programming—my fundamental role is to bridge the gap between advanced methodology and business reality. I operate not just as a vendor, but as an embedded partner, helping executive teams translate complex statistical uncertainty into actionable, strategic decisions.

Systems and Teams

A recurrent theme in my consulting work is that solving a data problem once is rarely enough; the solution must be scalable, maintainable, and owned by the business.

Beyond building bespoke models, I specialize in the “zero-to-one” phase of technical infrastructure. This includes:

  • Building Custom Tools: Developing proprietary R and Python packages (like internal forecasting engines) tailored to a company’s specific data architecture and business logic.

  • Mentoring Technical Talent: Upskilling in-house data science teams, establishing rigorous code review practices, and fostering a culture of reproducible research.

  • Architecting Causal Frameworks: Designing robust experimental and observational systems to ensure that when a company measures impact, they are capturing true causal effects, not just spurious correlations.

The Case for Probabilistic Programming

Standard machine learning often provides a single, highly optimized prediction. But in high-stakes environments—where the cost of being wrong is severe, or where the data is constrained by “long tail” distributions—point estimates are dangerous.

My work heavily leverages probabilistic programming (Stan, CmdStan) because it allows businesses to quantify uncertainty explicitly. Rather than giving a decision-maker a single number, I build models that provide a full distribution of possible outcomes. This allows organizations to build in regularization where data is sparse, evaluate risk mathematically, and give the business “levers and dials” they can trust.
